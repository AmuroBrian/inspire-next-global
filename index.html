ZAIBATSU MODEL
The foundational philosophy of the Zaibatsu model revolves around placing capital or the financial institution at the epicenter of its operations. Under this model, subsidiary and affiliated companies collaborate with external investors, political allies and affiliates, all united by a common objective: fostering capital expansion and augmenting their sphere of influence.
In essence, the Zaibatsu model is rooted in the strategic alignment of financial resources and relationships. It thrives on the synergy between a central financial entity and a network of associated businesses, all orchestrated to maximize wealth accumulation and wield substantial influence in both economic and political arenas. This intricate web of connections and interests amplifies the collective capacity for growth and leverage, making the Zaibatsu model a formidable force in the business world.
